Chapter 1

If one L1 decentralizes, what should happen to total cost?

This is the cleanest lens on the objection. Hold total network size fixed. Then ask whether moving from a tight validator set to a broader one should reduce total price, increase total price, or just change efficiency per validator.

This is where the monotonic and hybrid models become easier to defend than the pure Gaussian draft.

Chapter 2

Now keep one L1 fixed and let Avalanche mature.

This is the network-growth lens. The 10k region matters politically and economically, because that is where the original ACP-255 story has the most intuitive force, but also where reviewers start asking harder pricing questions.

The hybrid exists for this exact transition zone. It preserves the growth story, then gets stricter once that story becomes harder to justify.
